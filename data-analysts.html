Data Analysts

Welcome to the sandbox.

As a data analyst, you’ll receive a cloud-based workstation with preloaded programming environments and software so you can work within the USDOT Secure Data Commons (SDC). The workstations include access to the data lake and data warehouse. Also included are commercially available tools, without needing to install tools or software locally.

Data analysts can:

  • Share code and data with each other
  • Upload their own datasets
  • Export approved derived analysis

Datasets

The SDC platform provides a data lake of transportation-related datasets. The datasets consist of structured, semistructured, and unstructured data and are stored in raw, curated, and published formats. Each dataset has different data agreements based on the complexity and sensitivity of the data. Access to specific data is approved by data providers.

Dataset Formats

  • Raw Datasets
    Raw datasets are those in which their data are stored in their native or original format. These datasets can be structured (databases, logs, financial data), semistructured (HTML, XML, RDF, CSV), or unstructured (images, PDFs, Word documents). The data are unaltered and stored in as-is format. Uploads can be continuous through streaming sources, such as APIs or sensors, or through one-time uploads from external sources.
  • Curated
    Data curation is the process of integrating raw data collected from various sources, annotating the data, and presenting them so that the value of the data is maintained over time and the data remain available for reuse and preservation. For researchers and data scientists, curated datasets enable data discovery and retrieval and maintain data quality. During the curation process, data are transformed from unstructured and semistructured formats to structured formats; and data deduplication, obfuscation, and cleansing processes are conducted, thus providing high-quality data that enables researchers to elicit meaningful insights.
  • Published
    Researchers create published datasets to disclose their research and allow other users to verify and reuse the data beyond their original purpose. Published datasets are a result of combining analyses on curated datasets in the SDC platform with other datasets or algorithms owned or created by a researcher or data scientist.

Analytical Tools

The SDC platform provides on-demand access to popular programming and statistical tool packages for cloud-based processing (for experienced analysts). These include:

  • RStudio
  • Python
  • SQL

Other, nonstandard software can be installed upon request, both individually and across user groups. Analysts can use existing software licenses as well.

Current Projects

Available datasets on the SDC platform include the following:

Waze — Connected Citizens Program

The U.S. Department of Transportation’s (USDOT) Safety Data Initiative (SDI) is estimating police-reported traffic crashes in near real time by combining crowdsourced data from Waze with crash data provided by the State of Maryland via the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration’s Electronic Data Transfer pilot. Learn more about the SDI research.

Connected Vehicle Pilot Deployment Program

USDOT awarded cooperative agreements, collectively worth more than $45 million, to three pilot sites in New York City, Tampa, and Wyoming to implement a suite of connected vehicle applications and technologies tailored to meet the unique transportation needs of each site’s region. The SDC is a repository for select data generated by the deployments to support independent evaluations of the connected vehicle pilots. Test data from the Tampa and Wyoming pilots already are available in the SDC. As the deployments transition to the demonstration phase in 2019, data from all three sites will be available.

This dataset is currently available only to USDOT-funded researchers involved in the independent evaluation activities for the Connected Vehicle Pilot.

American Transportation Research Institute Freight Mobility Initiative Program

The USDOT Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS) is continually interested in understanding the extent and use of the Nation’s transportation network, the performance and impacts of the U.S. transportation system, and the flows of people, goods, and vehicles over the transportation network. This information is critical to support federal, state, and local policymaking and funding allocations.

The Freight Mobility Initiative (FMI) project within the SDC brings together data from the American Transportation Research Institute (ATRI) that leverages GPS on trucks with spatial statisticians from BTS to develop new approaches to analyze and visualize freight performance and network use.
