Waze — Connected Citizens Program

The U.S. Department of Transportation’s (USDOT) Safety Data Initiative (SDI) is a cross-cutting, collaborative effort within DOT, led by the Office of the Secretary of Transportation (OST). The intent of SDI is to build on and enhance safety efforts related to data, analysis, and policy making. With the Waze data SDI is integration transportation data to develop rapid crash indicators.

Connected Vehicle Pilot Deployment Program

USDOT awarded cooperative agreements (collectively worth more than $45 million) to three pilot sites in New York City, Tampa, and Wyoming to implement a suite of connected vehicle applications and technologies tailored to meet the unique transportation needs of each site’s region. The SDC is a repository for select data generated by the deployments to support independent evaluations of the connected vehicle pilots. Test data from the Tampa and Wyoming pilots are available in the SDC – check back soon for data from all three sites.

American Transportation Research Institute Freight Mobility Initiative Program

The USDOT Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS) is continually interested in understanding the extent and use of the Nation’s transportation network, the performance and impacts of the U.S. transportation system, and the flow of people, goods, and vehicles over the transportation network as this data is critical to supporting federal, state, and local policymaking and funding allocations. Within the SDC, the American Transportation Research Institute’s (ATRI) Freight Mobility Initiative (FMI) project brings together data from ATRI that leverages GPS on trucks with spatial statisticians from BTS to develop new approaches to analyze and visualize freight performance and network use.
